The "Arbel" Mountain

The Arbel is a cliff located in northern Israel in the eastern part of the Lower Galilee, northwest of Tiberias, north of the Arbel Valley. Mount Arbel rises to a height of 181 meters above sea level, about 390 meters above the Sea of Galilee.
The Arbel Cliff, which rises above the Sea of Galilee, overlooks the Golan heights of Hermon and the Galilee Mountains and is the heart of the Arbel Nature Reserve, and the Arbel National Park that surrounds it. The Arbel National Park and Nature Reserve also includes Mount Nitai, Mount Savyon, Nahal Arbel, as well as two ancient settlements that left the remains of two magnificent synagogues and a wall from the time of the Great Revolt, as well as hiking trails to an ancient fortress with natural caves.
